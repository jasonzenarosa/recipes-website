Laing

Taro leaves in coconut milk.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 lb. Pork Belly (Cut into small pieces)
  • 1 pack Dried Taro Leaves (~114g)
  • 1 can of Coconut Milk (preferably Mae Ploy brand)
  • 4 Garlic Cloves (Crushed and minced)
  • 1/2 Large Onion (Diced)
  • 2 thumb-sized knobs of Ginger (Peeled and sliced)
  • 5 Serrano Peppers
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Chicken or Pork Boullion (Optional)

Steps

  1. Add pork belly and 1/2 cup of water to a pan and turn the heat to medium. Stir often until it the fat renders and meat turns golden brown.
  2. Drain oil from pan, leaving about 2 tbsp. Push the pork to one side of the pan and add garlic, onion, and ginger to the other.
  3. Saute for 30 seconds and then stir together with the pork.
  4. Add the coconut milk, serrano peppers, salt, pepper, and pork/chicken boullion.
  5. Bring to a boil, stirring occasionally. Then, add the taro leaves.
  6. Stir in 1 cup of water. Cover and bring to a boil. Then, reduce heat to medium-low.
  7. Cover and simmer for 1-1.5 hours until soft and the liquid is reduced in half. Stir occasionally.
  8. Turn heat to medium for about 5 minutes or until the peppers are done and the liquid becomes thicker, stirring frequently to avoid burning the bottom.
  9. Serve!
